manufacturing process of bulk bags
step-1 febric

A piece of fabric is a material manufactured by weaving, knitting, spreading, felting, stitching, crocheting, or bonding that can be used to make other products, such as clothes and upholstery, and hence necessitates an additional stage in the manufacturing process.

step-2 designing

designing is the most important part in build bag because without design bags shape and look is not perfect so designing part is most important in manufacture process. in all types of bag designing process is must to show elegant bag in out out public.

step-3 cutting

The polypropylene fabric is fed into a slicing machine and cut into forms with precise dimensions. Whether the sides, edges, or bottoms are being generated during a specific operation determines the measurements. The measurements are taken in an automated procedure, ensuring precise lengths for each cut. The proper cuts are done at this stage, whether a line of bulk bags needs to be square, tubular, or vertically rectangular.

step-4 printing framing

The tapes formed from the PP granules are loaded onto weaving looms. Here, the polypropylene fabric that forms the body for a bulk bag is woven into shape. The resulting material is strong enough to carry a vast array of industrial products and consumable goods. However, it’s “breathable,” and thus subject to the passage of moisture. In addition to providing material for bulk bags, the woven PP material is also used for loading and discharge components.

step-5 printing

The polypropylene fabric pieces are fed into a printing machine after being trimmed into form. Fabric impressions are created here. Printing takes place at this step if a corporation wants to apply its brand name or logo to a range of bags. A variety of colors may be used, depending on the brand in question. A heavy-duty printer is required for this method to produce the best possible impressions.

step-6 stiching

An industrial sewing machine is used to sew together the various pieces that make up the bulk bag, including the square and rectangular polypropylene fabric pieces, as well as the heavier lifting-loop strips. A team of trained hands sews bulk bags together in large quantities under the supervision of technical supervisors. There must be no wrinkles or bends along either of the sides or bottom ends, and each seam must be straight, snug, and secure.

step-7 storage

With everything completed, the baled and packed bulk bags are sent to a storage room. The bags await their dispatch to various customer locations. Once received by a customer, the packed bulk bags are ready to be opened and put to use, whether the intended purpose is for the transportation or storage of building materials, rubble, food products or chemicals.
